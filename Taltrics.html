Taltrics Web Application

Over an intensive 8-week sprint, I undertook comprehensive research and design efforts for this multifaceted web application focusing on resource allocation. My role centered on conducting user interviews, usability testing and rapid prototyping refining the app's interface and functionality based on feedback. The process culminated in a meticulously designed solution ready for investors.

THE PROBLEM

Today, resource allocation is inefficient. It lacks real-time data support and is typically managed in a room where managers discuss and move people around on a spreadsheet. This inefficiency can result in increased operational costs and project delays.


THE PROJECT

Create a low-fidelity wireframe to revamp Taltrics' current resource allocation beta interface.


Beta - Resource Allocation

MAIN USER STORIES

The project centered around two primary user stories: Displaying supply and demand onto one interface, and incorporating AI recommendations into the workflow.


Main User Stories

USER INTERVIEWS

With our product's users being business leaders, scheduling their time posed challenges. Our project plan anticipated these challenges and accommodated their potential impact. The weekly user interviews yielded valuable insights that played a crucial role in our product's success.

Interview Findings - Affinity Map

DESIGN ITERATIONS

As an executive decision, I chose to introduce a 3-column layout that showcases the primary user story requirements and a drag & drop user flow that's easily understandable and learnable. As more research was gathered, the design iterations showed a continuous improvement.

USABILITY TESTING

With the prototype ready it was time to test. Because Taltrics' users are executives, they had very little time to spare to collect data. To pivot, our team opted for an unmoderated remote usability test on the prototype, utilizing Maze. (Test for portfolio purposes only; no data collection) View Test

ACHIEVEMENTS

Thanks to thorough and effective research, paired with sound design decisions and my proficiency in rapid prototyping, our team outpaced the schedule. By week 5, the client presented the design to investors, and by week 6, our design team collaborated with the engineer to construct the prototype within a sandbox environment.


CLOSING
